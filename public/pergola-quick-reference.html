General Installation Rules

Use these as the baseline before discussing product fit, site suitability, or installation method.

Critical

Surface & Level

  • Install only on a flat, level surface.
  • Pergolas cannot follow roofline contours.
  • Do not create a slope toward the house.
  • Sloped installation can jam blades, damage mechanisms, and cause leaks.

Drainage

  • All models use internal guttering.
  • Custom and insert installations may need multiple water exits.
  • Pergolas may leak until separate drainage is completed.
  • Customers should arrange plumbing for custom drainage work.

Operation

  • Do not force winding mechanisms or motors.
  • After rain, open blades slowly in small increments so water drains first.
  • For high wind exposure, leave blades slightly open when not in use.

Maintenance

  • Remove leaves, twigs, pine needles, and other debris from blades.
  • Debris can stop blades sealing and may cause dripping.
  • Mesh in post corners can help catch debris, but is not a standard factory feature.

Model Snapshot

A fast comparison of where each model fits and what staff should remember.

Product Range

Atlantic Manual

  • Entry-level pergola.
  • Freestanding or wall-mounted.
  • Not fully watertight.
  • Larger spans use multiple blade banks.
  • No-post configurations are not supported.

Caspian Electric

  • Mid-range electric option.
  • Integrated LED lighting in guttering.
  • Freestanding or wall-mounted.
  • Shares core structural specifications with Atlantic.

Tasman Premium

  • Suitable for wider spans without a centre post.
  • Heavy-duty blades and larger posts.
  • Integrated LED lights in blades.
  • Standard plug connection.

Pacific Commercial

  • For custom, awkward, or commercial projects.
  • Heavy-duty louvres and multicolour LEDs in guttering.
  • Expandable louvre bays for longer structures.
  • Requires electrician-wired controls.

Mediterranean Sliding

  • Commercial-style sliding roof.
  • Blades open upward and slide back.
  • Heavier-duty product, often selected for commercial environments.

Dova Retractable

  • Retractable louvred roof with integrated LED lights.
  • Roof retracts back by a portion of the opening.
  • Useful where a retractable roof helps with consent considerations.

Measuring Requirements

Collect these details before quoting or confirming the model.

Before Quote
  1. Width to outside of foot plate
  2. Depth
  3. Height to underside of pergola
  4. Maximum height with blades open

Photos

  • Collect site photos showing the installation area and surrounding structure.
  • Include nearby walls, roofline, fascia, gutters, deck, concrete, and access points.

Clearance & Voids

  • Allow generous clearance above the unit for structure variation and installation access.
  • For existing voids, measure to the smallest point and pack out to fit.
  • It is easier to pack out than to reduce an oversized unit.

Fixing & Structural Checks

Confirm the site can support the selected mounting approach before committing.

Risk Area

Decks & Concrete

  • On timber decks, bolts must fix into joists.
  • If joists are not available, add nogging or reinforcing timber.
  • On concrete, use suitable heavy-duty concrete fixings.
  • Posts may need cutting to account for patio slope while keeping the pergola level.

Fascia & Seat Brackets

  • Secure brackets into solid timber only.
  • Avoid fixing into unsupported fascia or the ends of roof trusses.
  • Some sites require gutter removal and reinforcement before fixing.
  • Complex mounting should be handled by the customer's builder.

Electrical Reference

Check the power requirement and who must complete the electrical work.

Electrical

Standard Requirements

  • Electrical components require a dedicated power line.
  • Do not share the pergola line with lower-load outdoor lighting.
  • Motors use a screw ram system.
  • Avoid settings that bend aluminium components.

Model Differences

  • Tasman includes a sealed plug attached to the cable.
  • Do not modify plugs.
  • Pacific requires a registered electrician to wire into its control box.
  • Commercial installations require registered electrical work.

Custom & No-Post Installations

Use extra care where the project depends on structural changes or site-specific drainage.

High Risk
No-post and insert-style work can create drainage and structural risks. The customer should use their own builder for complex mounting, waterproofing, and site-specific building tasks.

Supported With Care

  • Tasman can be used for custom no-post work when properly engineered.
  • Dimensions can be adjusted slightly during manufacturing for fit.
  • Plan water exits before finalising the job.

High-Risk Areas

  • Atlantic no-post configurations are not supported.
  • Removing a leg can create leakage risk if drainage is not managed.
  • Water ingress and waterproof membrane damage can create major repair costs.

Accessories

Common add-ons and the installation notes that matter most.

Add-ons

Walls & Panels

  • Slat walls.
  • Tongue-and-groove panels.
  • Louvre panel options.

Blinds

  • Manual blinds have limited width and need posts for attachment.
  • Electric blinds can span wider openings.
  • Electric blinds integrate with compatible remote and hub systems.
  • Outside the pergola structure, each blind may need its own hub box and power point.

Doors

  • Bifold and sliding doors are available in glass or louvred styles.
  • Doors are heavy and require perfectly flat, level surfaces.
  • Sloped patios are unsuitable because packing can look unfinished.

Customer Expectation Checklist

A short final scan before quote confirmation or installation handoff.

Checklist

Before Quoting

  • Confirm model suitability for span, mounting type, and site complexity.
  • Collect all required measurements and site photos.
  • Check whether the surface is flat and level.
  • Confirm if drainage, plumbing, electrical, or building work is required.

Before Installation

  • Confirm payment and installation requirements are complete.
  • Make sure drainage expectations have been explained.
  • Make sure the customer understands third-party builder or electrician requirements.
  • Use line drawings and manuals as the main technical reference.
