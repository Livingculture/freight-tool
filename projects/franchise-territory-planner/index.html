How the purchaser journey works

A clean process builds trust. Each step should give the buyer enough information to proceed while protecting commercial, legal and territory decisions until qualification is complete.

1

Initial enquiry

Buyer enters their city, preferred radius, available capital and contact details.

2

Territory check

Population, catchment size and existing showroom overlap are reviewed.

3

Discovery call

Discuss fit, experience, capital, values, and whether the area is available.

4

Financial review

Candidate reviews turnover, cost, profit, cash-flow and sensitivity assumptions.

5

Documentation

Issue franchise documents, territory map and launch obligations for professional advice.

6

Launch plan

Site selection, showroom fitout, product training, trade launch and local marketing.

What buyers should understand before proceeding

  • A territory is not just a circle on a map. It should consider population, accessibility, competitor presence, trade density, existing showrooms and logistics.
  • More than one store may make sense when the population exceeds the people-per-store threshold, but rollout timing should be staged.
  • Numbers should be treated as a feasibility guide until the franchisor approves real costs, margins, royalties and territory terms.
  • Candidates should obtain independent legal and accounting advice before signing any franchise or territory agreement.
